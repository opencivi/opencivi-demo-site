Our Story

In 1987, Rosa Marchetti opened a small kitchen on Elm Street with one goal: to share the food she grew up cooking in Naples with her new hometown. What started as a 12-table diner has grown into a beloved neighborhood staple — but the recipes haven't changed one bit.

Today, Rosa's daughter Maria and son-in-law Tony run the kitchen together, using the same handwritten recipe book Rosa brought from Italy. Every sauce is made from scratch. Every pasta is rolled by hand. Every dessert is baked on-site.

We don't do takeout. We don't do delivery. We believe good food deserves to be eaten at a table, with people you love.

— Mama Rosa & Family
